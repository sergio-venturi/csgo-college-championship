# University CS:GO Championship Rules

## Objective

The objective of the University CS:GO Championship is to promote healthy competition and camaraderie among university students, as well as encourage the development of teamwork, strategy, and leadership skills.

If you wish to contact the Academic Center team with questions or comments, feel free to send an email to caengcompunivali@gmail.com, with the subject "CS:GO Rules."

### Eligibility

1. All registered players must be enrolled at the University of Vale do Itaja√≠ in higher education (university professors are also accepted).

2. Graduates will be accepted if the number of teams does not reach its maximum.

3. The maximum number of teams is set at sixteen (16) teams.

## Registration

1. Registrations for the University CS:GO Championship will be exclusively done through an online form provided by the event organizers.

2. To register, interested teams must fill out the form with accurate and authentic information. Any falsification or omission of information may result in disqualification.

3. The team must pay a registration fee of R$ 50.00 via pix or cash to the organization.

4. Forms submitted without paying the registration fee will be disregarded.

5. Registrations will open in the afternoon on August 2nd (Wednesday) and close at midnight on August 9th (Wednesday).

6. The event organizers reserve the right to close the registration period before the established deadline if the maximum number of teams has already been reached.

## Tournament Format

1. The championship will be held in a single-elimination format, where teams play matches against each other until a winner is determined.

2. Matches will be played in a best-of-one format during the tournament, with semifinals and finals in a best-of-three format.

3. In case of a tie during a match, an overtime period will be played to determine the winner. The overtime period will be played in a best-of-six rounds format.

4. Match times may be advanced, and it is the team's responsibility to attend at the new time. Delays will not be tolerated.

5. The event organizers reserve the right to change the tournament format in case of necessity or unforeseen circumstances.

## Maps

1. Maps will be chosen through a veto system. Each team will have the right to ban three maps, and the remaining map will be played.

2. The available maps are:
   - Ancient;
   - Anubis;
   - Inferno;
   - Mirage;
   - Nuke;
   - Overpass;
   - Vertigo.

## Communication

1. Team communication must be done through the server provided on Discord. The access link to the server will be provided to team captains, who must pass it on to their respective teams.

## Peripherals

1. Players may bring their own peripherals for use during tournament matches, such as keyboard, mouse, mousepad, and headset.

2. Peripherals must be in good working condition and must not offer unfair advantages to players.

3. The event organizers reserve the right to inspect players' peripherals before the start of the match to ensure they comply with the rules.

4. If a peripheral malfunctions during the match, the player may request a pause to replace it. The pause must be approved by the match judges, and the player will have a time limit to make the replacement.

5. The event organizers are not responsible for lost, damaged, or stolen peripherals during the tournament. It is the responsibility of each player to ensure the security of their own peripherals.

## Formation Rules

1. Each team must maintain five (5) players in the main lineup ("Starting Team") for the entire duration of the tournament, and a maximum of two (2) substitutes ("Reserves"), and optionally, one (1) coach, forming the ("Active Team").

2. No individual can simultaneously occupy more than one role listed above.

3. A team may be composed of players from different courses.

4. The use of players not registered in the team is prohibited. If a team uses an unregistered player, the team will be disqualified.

5. The team lineup must be established before the start of the tournament and cannot be changed after the start of the matches.

6. A player is not allowed to play for more than one team.

7. Each team may designate a coach, and the following rules apply:
   - The coach cannot be a starting player or a reserve;
   - Each coach can only represent one team;
   - The entry of coaches is subject to future changes (teams will be duly informed in case of changes).

8. The team formation is the exclusive responsibility of the team. The event organizers are not responsible for any problems related to team formation.

9. Changes in the starting lineup can occur according to the section **Player Substitution**.

## Identification

1. All players from participating teams must present a photo identification document upon entering the location of each match.

2. The following identification documents will be accepted:
   - Identity card (RG);
   - Driver's license (CNH);
   - Passport.

3. If a player does not present a valid photo identification document, they will not be allowed to participate in the matches.

4. The event organizers reserve the right to verify the authenticity of the documents presented by the players.

5. In case of any suspicion of forgery or irregularity in the players' identification, the event organizers may disqualify the team or player in question.

6. On-site identification is a mandatory requirement to ensure the safety and integrity of the championship.

## Player Substitution

1. Teams are allowed to make the following changes to their Active Teams:
   - Change of player from Reserve to Starting Team;
   - Change of player from Starting Team to Reserve;
   - Inclusion of new players during the championship is not allowed;
   - Dismissal of players during the championship is not allowed;
   - Player substitutions by reserves can only be made at the beginning of the match with staff present. After the start of the match, teams cannot change their Line-Up until the start of the next match.

## Role of Judges

1. The judges are responsible for assisting players in any matter related to the matches and what happens before and after the matches. Some of their responsibilities include:
   - Ensuring that the championship matches are conducted according to the established rules;
   - The judges will be appointed by the event organizers, and their decision will be final and unquestionable;
   - During the matches, the judges have the authority to apply penalties for rule violations, such as punishments for cheating or unsportsmanlike behavior;
   - In case of doubt, judges may request a review of plays through tools available in the game or consult the event organizers;
   - Judges cannot interfere with the progress of the match unless there is a clear violation of the rules;
   - The judges are responsible for ensuring the safety and integrity of the championship. In case of any incident, the judges must take the necessary measures to ensure the safety of players and spectators;
   - The decisions of the judges must be respected by all teams and players, under penalty of disqualification from the championship.

2. The judge must behave professionally at all times and must make decisions impartially and without passion or prejudice towards any player, team, coach, sponsor

, or individual.

## Player Conduct

1. Players must respect the game rules and the judges' decisions in all matches.

2. The use of any form of cheating or unsportsmanlike conduct is prohibited, such as:

   - **Collusion:** Defined as any agreement between two (2) or more players and/or teams intended to harm another team or player;
   - **Competitive Integrity:** Teams are expected to play their best at all times during the tournament and avoid any behavior incompatible with the principles of sportsmanship and fair play. For clarification purposes, team composition and the pick/ban phase will not be considered when analyzing whether this rule has been violated or not;
   - **Hacking:** Hacking is defined as any alteration or modification made to the Counter Strike client by a player, team, or any person acting on behalf of a player or team;
   - **Account Sharing:** Playing on another player's account or requesting, inducing, encouraging someone to play on another player's account;
   - **Cheating Programs:** The use of any tool or program for cheating purposes;
   - **Intentional Disconnection:** Disconnecting from a game without reason or motive;
   - **Offenses and Hate Speech:** Team members cannot use obscene, vulgar, insulting, threatening, abusive, slanderous, defamatory language, or behave in any offensive or reprehensible manner, nor promote or incite hatred or discriminatory conduct;
   - **Disruptive/Insulting Behavior:** Team members cannot take any action, perform any gesture, or incite any individual to do the same, directed at the opposing team in an insulting, mocking, or disturbing nature.

3. The consumption of alcoholic beverages or drugs during the championship is prohibited. If a player is caught consuming such substances, the team will be disqualified.

4. Players must attend matches at the time established by the event organizers. Delays will not be tolerated, and the team may be penalized.

5. Players must keep the environment clean and organized, and it is not allowed to leave trash or litter in the common areas of the event.

6. Damaging or causing any kind of harm to the event facilities is prohibited. If a player causes damage, the team will be responsible for paying for the repairs.

## Prizes

1. The championship prizes will be announced before the start of the tournament.

2. Prizes will be awarded only to teams that comply with all the rules established in this regulation.

3. The winning team of the tournament will receive the largest portion of the prize, with the amount divided among the players according to the previous agreement among them.

4. Other placements will receive smaller prizes, also divided among the players of each team.

5. The event organizers reserve the right to change the prizes at any time, provided that it is justified and communicated to the participating teams in advance.

6. The winning teams will be announced and awarded at the end of the tournament. It is the responsibility of each team to be present at the awards ceremony to receive their prize.

## Final Dispositions

1. The event organizers reserve the right to change any item in this regulation at any time if deemed necessary.

2. Any situation not foreseen in this regulation will be evaluated by the event organizers, who will make the best decision to ensure the fairness and transparency of the tournament.

3. By registering for the University CS:GO Championship, teams agree to fully comply with this regulation and all decisions made by the event organizers.

4. This regulation is valid for the second half of the year two thousand and twenty-three (2023).
